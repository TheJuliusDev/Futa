The 75:25 Formula Explained

Step 1: JAMB Component (75%)

JAMB Points = (JAMB Score ÷ 400) × 75

Your UTME score is scaled to 75 points. A score of 400 gives a perfect 75.00.

Step 2: O'Level Component (25%)

O'Level Points = (Sum of 5 Grades ÷ 5 ÷ 100) × 25

Grade values: A1=80, B2=72, B3=67, C4=62, C5=57, C6=52. Average is taken, then scaled to 25 points.

Step 3: Final Aggregate

Aggregate = JAMB Points + O'Level Points

Maximum possible aggregate = 75.00 + 25.00 = 100.00

Worked Example

Given:
JAMB Score:280
English Language:A1 (80)
Mathematics:B2 (72)
Physics:B3 (67)
Chemistry:C4 (62)
Biology:C5 (57)
1
JAMB: (280 ÷ 400) × 75 = 0.7 × 75 = 52.50
2
O'Level Sum: 80+72+67+62+57 = 338
Average = 338 ÷ 5 = 67.6
(67.6 ÷ 100) × 25 = 16.90
3
Aggregate: 52.50 + 16.90 = 69.40

Admission Tips for FUTA Aspirants

Minimum JAMB Score

FUTA requires a minimum JAMB score of 180 to qualify for Post-UTME screening. Aim for 220+ for competitive departments.

O'Level Strategy

All 5 A1s give a perfect 20/25 O'Level score. Even C6s add meaningful points — don't leave any subject blank.

Competitive Departments

EEE (74.37), MEE (73.75), ARC (72.90), CVE (72.66) and MBBS (78.25) are the most competitive. Plan your combinations wisely.

Change of Course

If your aggregate is below your first-choice cutoff, use this calculator to find related departments where your score qualifies.

Stay Updated

Cutoff marks change yearly based on applicant performance. Always check futa.edu.ng for official updates.

Post-UTME Screening

FUTA uses Post-UTME (Computer-Based Test) scores alongside your JAMB and O'Level to compute this aggregate.
